Moin (MO-in)

In odd depths of jungles, damp cave entrances, and sandy mesa canyons, live the Moin. These people are alien to the larger societies of the world, and seldom seen by them. Most of the Moin live together in colonies, of which there are perhaps a few across the continent.

Moins speak their own simple language. Their colonies are actually impressive and complex, having systems of rulership and government, worship, games and performances, and social classes within a single colony.

Now and then an outsider may stumble across a Moin society, and be met with varying reactions depending on circumstance. Generally, though, many Moins are suspicious of outsiders, though some who are more accustomed to the presence of other races are more welcoming. Even more rarely, though, one of their members will leave their colony. This is usually temporary, but it has happened that a Moin has left their colony to be part of another society. Moins are fully capable of learning and speaking Common, though they prefer to keep their sentences and grammar simple, like their native language. From the perspective of a “civilized” person, Moins are odd in behavior, manners, and thought, and are often on completely different wavelengths than other people. As a result, though, Moins tend to think in more creative, abstract ways, and offer completely unexpected ideas, regardless of how good they might be.

Moins resemble hummins, generally having broader heads and bodies, skin color varying from sand to bronze or dark brown, depending on where they live. Body weight varies extensively among Moins, primarily based on one’s social status, and therefore, how much they eat.
